# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Snaproom is used by homeowners, interior designers, renovation consultants, and small architecture teams.
They use it while planning a build or remodel, often from a laptop at home or in a design review meeting.
Their core job is to validate room layout, furniture fit, and circulation before construction starts.

## Product Purpose
Snaproom turns a flat floor plan, hand-drawn sketch, or room image into a walkable 3D world.
It pipelines world generation, object mesh generation, and ambient sound from a single source image set.
Success means users can quickly trust what the space will feel like and make layout decisions with confidence.

## Brand Personality
Practical, spatial, confident.
The tone is clear and technical enough for professionals, but still welcoming for homeowners.
The experience should feel crafted and intentional, not playful gimmick UI.

## Anti-references
- Generic AI SaaS chrome with neon gradients and flashy empty claims.
- Over-gamified dashboards that hide core actions behind decorative effects.
- Dense enterprise forms that make upload and review workflows feel bureaucratic.

## Design Principles
- Show spatial confidence first: lead with outcomes users care about, then reveal implementation depth.
- Keep the generation journey legible: users should understand where they are in the pipeline at every moment.
- Favor tactile structure over decoration: typography, spacing, and hierarchy do more work than effects.
- Keep friction low for first runs: one clear primary action on each step.
- Preserve trust through transparency: reference providers and disk-first behavior in plain language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA for all UI surfaces.
Maintain readable contrast in all panels and controls.
Ensure keyboard focus visibility for actions and toggles.
Avoid motion-heavy transitions and support reduced cognitive load with clear status labels.
